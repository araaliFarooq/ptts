How Does GPS Vehicle Tracking Work?

Below, find information regarding the mechanics of GPS vehicle tracking, as well as the primary differences in vehicle tracking methods. As always, contact us with any questions you have regarding GPS vehicle tracking systems, software and devices.

How Does GPS Work?

The simple answer is that orbiting satellites watch your vehicle from above. However, it’s obviously a bit more complex than that. According the U.S. Department of Defense, 24 satellites orbit the Earth and transmit signals to the ground (additional satellites are on standby but are not actively in use). Each orbits the planet every 12 hours, and they are strategically placed for impressively accurate triangulation.

What Is Triangulation?

To fully understand how GPS tracking works, you should have a basic understanding of triangulation. In short, this method requires at least three active satellites but can view as many as twelve simultaneously. On the ground, a gps tracking device receives signals from the GPS satellites whereby each satellite knows the exact distance from the other satellites in its proximity. Depending on the time it takes for a signal to reach the device from each satellite, the GPS receiver can calculate it’s exact location on the ground.

How Precise Is GPS?

Extremely. Considering the distance a signal must travel – from space – the accuracy of GPS tracking is remarkable. If you have ever used a handheld GPS device, you know that your tracking dot can pinpoint whether you are at an intersection or between two blocks. GPS devices can tell your location within just a few dozen feet. The accuracy of the GPS device depends largely on atmospheric conditions, the speed you are traveling, your location and the type of GPS vehicle tracking device you are using – which brings us to our next point.

Cellular vs. Satellite Tracking Devices

Now that you understand the basics of how GPS works, we can expand on the differences between cellular and satellite tracking devices. This boils down to the type of wireless network that best suits your purposes.

Cellular Vehicle Tracking. This type of tracking is typically used in urban areas, or areas with reliable cellular network coverage. Depending on your region, different wireless companies, such as Airtel, MTN and UTL, can provide you with better coverage options.

Satellite Vehicle Tracking. Satellite tracking is preferable for Vehicles traveling in rural areas, or vehicles traveling in and out of reliable coverage zones.
